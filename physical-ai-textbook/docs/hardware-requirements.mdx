# Hardware Requirements: Building Your Physical AI Laboratory

This comprehensive guide outlines the hardware infrastructure needed to establish a world-class Physical AI laboratory. The requirements span simulation workstations, edge computing platforms, robotic hardware, and supporting infrastructure, with detailed cost analysis and implementation strategies.

## üñ•Ô∏è Core Simulation Workstation (Required per Student)

The foundation of any Physical AI curriculum is high-performance simulation capability. NVIDIA Isaac Sim and Gazebo demand substantial computational resources for real-time physics simulation and photorealistic rendering.

### GPU Specifications: The Critical Bottleneck

| Specification | Minimum | Recommended | Premium | Cost Range |
|---------------|---------|-------------|---------|------------|
| **GPU Model** | RTX 4070 Ti | RTX 4080 | RTX 4090 | $1,200 - $2,000 |
| **VRAM** | 12 GB | 16 GB | 24 GB | - |
| **CUDA Cores** | 7,680 | 9,728 | 16,384 | - |
| **Performance** | Basic Isaac Sim | Smooth workflows | Complex scenes | - |

**Why RTX Series?** Isaac Sim requires RTX-specific ray tracing capabilities for real-time rendering of complex USD scenes. Integrated laptop GPUs cannot handle the computational load.

### Complete Workstation Build

| Component | Specification | Purpose | Cost |
|-----------|---------------|---------|------|
| **CPU** | Intel i7-13700K / AMD Ryzen 9 7950X | Physics simulation, compilation | $400-600 |

| **Case + Cooling** | Mid-tower with good airflow | Thermal management | $100-150 |
| **Monitor** | 27" 1440p 144Hz | Development display | $300-400 |
| **OS** | Ubuntu 22.04 LTS | Native ROS 2 support | Free |

**Total Cost per Workstation: $2,600 - $3,300**

### Scaling Considerations

- **Student-to-Workstation Ratio**: 1:1 for intensive labs, 2:1 for standard coursework
- **Shared Resources**: Network storage for large datasets and simulation assets
- **Maintenance Budget**: 10-15% annually for upgrades and repairs

## üí∞ Budget Optimization Strategies

### Phased Implementation
1. **Phase 1**: Core workstations and edge kits ($20,000)
2. **Phase 2**: Robotic hardware ($10,000)
3. **Phase 3**: Advanced equipment as funding allows

### Cost Reduction Techniques
- **Bulk Purchasing**: 20%+ savings on multiple units
- **Refurbished Equipment**: 30-50% off for workstations
- **Shared Resources**: Maximize hardware utilization
- **Cloud Hybrid**: Mix on-premise and cloud resources

### Funding Sources
- **Educational Grants**: NSF, DARPA robotics programs
- **Industry Partnerships**: Sponsorship from robotics companies
- **Alumni Donations**: Targeted fundraising campaigns
- **Government Programs**: STEM education initiatives

## üîß Maintenance and Upgrades

### Annual Budget Allocation
- **Hardware Refresh**: 15-20% of initial investment
- **Software Licenses**: $5,000-10,000 for specialized tools
- **Consumables**: $2,000-5,000 for sensors, cables
- **Training**: $3,000-8,000 for faculty development

### Upgrade Timeline
- **Year 1-2**: Core stability, minor upgrades
- **Year 3**: Major workstation refresh
- **Year 4-5**: Complete lab renovation
